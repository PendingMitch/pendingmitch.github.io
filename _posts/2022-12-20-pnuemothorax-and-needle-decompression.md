### So... Simply

Tension pneumothorax is the gathering of air between two thin membranes that line and surround the lungs. This compresses the lungs and restricts the volume of blood that can return to the heart. This in turn reduces the cardiac output.

## Needle Decompression. What is it?

Sometimes called "Needle Thoracostomy", is insertion of a needle into the pleural space to decompress a tension pneumothorax.

## Needle Decompression. How does it work?

The insertion of a needle allows air to escape the cavity. This will relieve pressure from the pleural space and will allow the full inflation and deflation of lungs and subsequently the heart to return to normal cardiac capacity.

## Needle Decompression. How to perform it.

### Needed Equipment Checklist

- 14- or 16-gauge needle. 8-cm needles are more successful but risk injury to underlying structures.
- PPE - Gloves, etc.
- Cleansing solution - such as 2% chlorhexidine solution

### Considerations before performing

- Urgency of the procedure. Can this procedure be delayed, to be dealt with in a sterile environment such as a hospital?
- Creating further injury

- Patient should be lying on their back, on a flat surface.
