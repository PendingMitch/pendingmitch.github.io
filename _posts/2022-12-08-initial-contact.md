## What is initial contact?

The initial contact is one of the most important parts of the treatment process. It involves understanding the scene, the individuals in the scene and any other information that it involves.

## Information to collect from scene.

METHANE is the accronym that we use to analyse scenes.

| Letter | Meaning                                                                 |
| ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| M      | Major Incident? If not continue with ETHANE.                            |
| E      | Exact location of the incident. (Typically identified with What3Words). |
| T      | Type of incident.                                                       |
| H      | Any (potential) hazards that can be identified                          |
| A      | The best access route to the incident                                   |
| N      | Number of casualties                                                    |
| E      | Emergency Services. Are any other assets of services needed?            |

## Information to collect from patient.

| Information                               | Why?                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Name                                      | This should be gathered to ensure that the patient recieves a decent level of care within the time they spend with our crews. It's not _just_ about the medical care, it's about the personal care too. |
| Age                                       | Age will determine how you deal with the subject. If they are older you may understand that there is more risk to certain actions as others.                                                            |
| Outstanding / Previous Medical Conditions | Medical conditions will determine how we deliver our care. Decisions may be made differently if there are reasons to believe the medical conditions may complicate the symptoms                         |
| Allergies                                 | Allergies will prevent you from delivering certain treatments. Remember: we're trying not to kill our patients                                                                                          |
| Medications                               | Some medications may cause different effects that expected if other drugs are present within the patient's system                                                                                       |
