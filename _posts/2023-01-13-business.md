## 2.1 - Raising Finance

Businesses need finance in order to buy fixed assets like, for example, premises or machinery.\
They need to consider

-   Amount required,
-   Level of risk involved, and
-   Cost of the finance

### Differences between source and method

A **source** of finance is the provider. The **method** is the **way** in which the provider gives the finance.\
For example, a bank is a source, whereas their overdraft is the method.

### 2.1.1 - Internal Finance

Internal finance can be raised by using the owner's money, selling assets, or putting profits back into the business. It's typically related to organic growth.

#### Owner's Capital

-   Money that is owned by the owner(s)
-   Most likely used by sole traders or partnerships
-   It is easy to access
-   But, the amount that can be raised is often limited.

#### Selling Assets

-   Can only be used for businesses with spare assets meaning that it's not viable for small or new businesses
-   It's a cheap method, but they would no longer own the assets which may lower maximum capacity

#### Retained proft

-   Using profit from previous periods after taxation and dividends
-   A cheap method of financing
-   But, shareholders may reject this source as it eats into their dividends.

Retained profit is often used in combination with other methods to fund expansion.

### 2.1.2 - External Finance

External financing is money raised by a company from outside sources.

| Source           | Method                         | Benefits / Drawback                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Family & Friends | Loan / Gift / Donation         | * Will be more willing to give a flexible repayment plan * But the amount of money will often likely only be small                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Banks            | Loans / Overdrafts / Mortgages | * Terms and conditions of each product are clear * And the business is more likely to be able to get advise on each of the products * But, they have strict credit criteria which may mean that it can be hard for start-ups or risky businesses. * And banks will have interest rates. |
